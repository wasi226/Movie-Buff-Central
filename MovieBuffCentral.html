Animal is a 2023 Indian Hindi-language action drama film[1][5] co-written, directed and edited by Sandeep Reddy Vanga and produced by T-Series Films, Bhadrakali Pictures and Cine1 Studios. The film stars Ranbir Kapoor, Anil Kapoor, Bobby Deol, Rashmika Mandanna and Triptii Dimri. The film follows Ranvijay, the ruthless son of a powerful industrialist, and his troubled relationship with his father, which gets further jeopardized as he sets out on a path of vengeance and destruction after an assassination attempt on his father. The film was officially announced in January 2021. Principal photography began in April 2022 and wrapped by April 2023, with cinematography by Amit Roy. The film's soundtrack album was composed by Pritam, JAM8, Vishal Mishra, Jaani, Manan Bhardwaj, Shreyas Puranik, Ashim Kemson and Harshavardhan Rameshwar. With a runtime of 201 minutes, Animal is one of the longest Indian films ever made. It was initially set for an August 2023 release but was postponed due to post-production work.[6]

Shiddat is a 2021 Hindi-language romantic drama that explores the intensity and power of love. Directed by Kunal Deshmukh and produced by T-Series and Maddock Films, the movie tells the story of two passionate lovers whose lives are deeply intertwined by fate. The film stars Sunny Kaushal as Jaggi, a free-spirited young man with a burning desire for adventure and love, and Radhika Madan as Kartika, a beautiful and ambitious woman who is engaged to someone else. The plot revolves around Jaggi’s undying love for Kartika. After meeting her, he becomes convinced that they are destined to be together, despite Kartika’s impending marriage. His journey takes him across countries and obstacles, pushing him to pursue his dream of winning Kartika’s heart. With themes of sacrifice, fate, and unrelenting passion, Shiddat explores what one is willing to do in the name of true love. Supported by Mohit Raina and Diana Penty in key roles, Shiddat boasts a powerful soundtrack, with emotional songs that resonate with the characters’ journeys. The movie received praise for its passionate performances, stunning cinematography, and an emotional core that showcases the raw nature of love and desire.

Malaal is a 2019 Hindi-language romantic drama directed by Mangesh Hadawale, marking the debut of Meezaan Jaffery and Sharmin Segal in lead roles. Set in the backdrop of Mumbai's chawls, the film beautifully captures the essence of local life and the complexities of youthful love. Meezaan plays Shiva More, a carefree, impulsive, and aggressive young man who is struggling to find his way in life. Sharmin plays Astha Tripathi, a refined and educated girl from a well-off North Indian family who has recently moved to the neighborhood. The story unfolds as Shiva and Astha, despite their starkly different personalities and backgrounds, are drawn to each other. What begins as animosity slowly turns into a deep emotional connection. However, their relationship faces numerous challenges—class differences, social pressures, and personal insecurities—that force them to confront their feelings and life choices. Malaal explores themes of love, sacrifice, and the cultural divide, emphasizing how love can emerge in the most unexpected places. The film's strength lies in its raw and heartfelt portrayal of emotions, supported by soulful music composed by Sanjay Leela Bhansali, Shreyas Puranik, and Shail Hada. It’s a poignant reflection on the impact of love and life’s unpredictable nature.

Dear Zindagi is a 2016 Hindi-language coming-of-age drama directed by Gauri Shinde, known for its refreshing take on mental health, self-discovery, and emotional well-being. The film stars Alia Bhatt as Kaira, a talented yet conflicted cinematographer struggling with unresolved emotional issues, career dilemmas, and complex relationships. Kaira’s life takes a turn when she meets Dr. Jehangir "Jug" Khan, played by Shah Rukh Khan, a free-spirited and unconventional therapist who helps her navigate her inner turmoil. The movie beautifully portrays Kaira’s journey as she learns to confront her fears, childhood trauma, and commitment issues. Through her therapy sessions with Jug, she begins to understand the importance of self-love, forgiveness, and embracing imperfections. The film is not just about romantic relationships but also explores themes of family dynamics, friendships, and societal pressures. With its poignant storytelling, Dear Zindagi strikes a balance between light-hearted moments and deep emotional reflections. The film’s appeal lies in its relatability, showcasing that everyone, at some point, grapples with personal insecurities and seeks a path to healing.
